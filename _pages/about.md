# About Me

I am a student researcher in the SSE Bachelor-PhD program at The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen (CUHK-Shenzhen), where I am pursuing a Bachelor of Engineering in Computer Engineering (Expected July 2027). 

My core research interest and intended Ph.D. direction centers on **lithium-ion battery health diagnostics**, with a specific focus on **State of Health (SOH) and Remaining Useful Life (RUL) prediction models**. Currently, I serve as a Student Researcher at the AI Power Laboratory at CUHK-Shenzhen, supervised by Professor Junhua Zhao and Dr. Guolong Liu. My primary work investigates the challenges of early-cycle battery lifetime prediction under heterogeneous protocols and chemistries. To address feature competition between protocol priors and early degradation trajectories, I proposed an FWL-based two-stage residual learning framework that explicitly separates macro protocol effects from micro degradation signals. Furthermore, I developed a safety-aware prognostics framework utilizing leaf-space residual correction and asymmetric uncertainty estimation to reduce systematic lifetime overestimation for short-life batteries. This framework has been rigorously validated across four heterogeneous public datasets, successfully reducing the prediction error from 16.49% to 9.54% MAPE across 270 battery-disjoint evaluation windows.

Prior to concentrating on time-series prognostics and energy systems, I built a robust algorithmic foundation in **Computer Vision and Generative AI**:
* **Video Segmentation Consistency:** At the Future Network of Intelligence Institute, I utilized the SAM model, PCA analysis, and clustering techniques to resolve semantic information inconsistencies across multiple views in 2D video segmentation[cite: 2]. This research resulted in the development of MastSAM—achieving a 92.32% consistency on the DAVIS dataset—and BYOCL, both of which I authored as the first author.
* **4D Scene Generation:** I trained the VideoX-FUN model to enable dynamic indoor scene synthesis from textual descriptions and developed a 2D-to-4D generation pipeline integrating MEGA-SAM for depth-aware 3D reconstruction.
* **Industrial AI Applications:** As a Research Assistant at the Chinese Academy of Science, Cloud Computing Center, I transferred the Segment Anything Model (SAM) into rock blasting scenarios, building a rock volume and surface area estimation model using 3D point cloud data.

I am passionate about leveraging deep learning architectures to solve complex, real-world engineering problems, particularly in developing highly accurate, cross-domain generalization models.
